## Module 5: Biodiversity & Ecosystem Protection

## Section 1: Forest Conservation Importance

- **Lesson Content:**
    
    Forests house a huge variety of plants and animals, and provide clean air and water. Conserving forests prevents soil erosion and protects habitats. Deforestation disrupts balance.
    
- **Fun Fact:**
    
    Rainforests are home to more than half of Earth’s species!
    
- **Learn More:**
    
    [Wikipedia: Forest Conservation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_conservation)

## Section 2: Endangered Species Protection

- **Lesson Content:**
    
    Many animals and plants are now endangered due to habitat loss, pollution, and hunting. Conservation groups create reserves and laws to save them.
    
- **Example:**
    
    Tigers, elephants, rhinos, and pandas are all endangered.
    
- **Learn More:**
    
    [Wikipedia: Endangered Species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endangered_species)
    
- **Image:** Iconic endangered animals

---

## Section 3: Food Web Interactions

- **Lesson Content:**
    
    Every organism in an ecosystem is linked—plants are eaten by herbivores, which are eaten by carnivores. Disruptions, like pollution or extinction, affect the entire food web.
    
- **Fact:**
    
    Removing one species can impact many others!
    
- **Learn More:**
    
    [Wikipedia: Food Web](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_web)
    
- **Image:** Simple food web diagram

## Section 4: Human Impact on Ecosystems

- **Lesson Content:**
    
    Urbanization, pollution, and climate change alter habitats and endanger biodiversity. Understanding our impact helps us make better choices.
    
- **Example:**
    
    Littering and construction can destroy nesting sites for birds.
    
- **Learn More:**
    
    [Wikipedia: Human Impact on the Environment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment)

## Section 5: How Can You Protect Biodiversity?

- **Lesson Content:**
    - Participate in tree-planting drives
    - Reduce waste and pollution
    - Support wildlife conservation groups
    - Spread awareness
        
        Every small action adds up together!
